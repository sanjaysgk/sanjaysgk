# Immunolyser: Publishing My First Research Paper

Getting a paper published feels different than I expected. There's no fanfare — just a quiet email saying your manuscript has been accepted. But the journey to get there was anything but quiet.

## What is Immunolyser?

Immunolyser is an automated web-based pipeline for immunopeptidomics data analysis. It standardises what was previously a fragmented, manual process — bringing together peptide length distribution, motif analysis, sequence clustering (via GibbsCluster), and MHC binding affinity prediction (via NetMHCpan) into a single reproducible workflow.

## Why It Matters

Immunopeptidomics — the study of peptides presented by MHC molecules on cell surfaces — is critical for understanding immune responses and developing vaccines. But the data analysis landscape was a mess:

- Every lab had their own scripts
- Results weren't reproducible
- New researchers faced a steep learning curve

Immunolyser wraps the entire workflow into a web interface. Upload your data, select your parameters, get publication-ready figures.

## The Publication Journey

The paper was submitted to **Computational and Structural Biotechnology Journal (CSBJ)**, a peer-reviewed open-access journal. The review process took about 3 months:

1. **Initial submission** — June 2025
2. **First review** — Major revisions requested (additional validation, comparison with existing tools)
3. **Revision** — Added murine dataset validation, expanded methods section
4. **Acceptance** — October 2025

The reviewers pushed us to make the paper significantly better. Their feedback on validating across species (human and murine datasets) strengthened our claims considerably.

## Technical Lessons

- **Web-based tools have higher adoption** — Researchers prefer clicking to command-line
- **Reproducibility is a feature** — Every analysis run gets a unique ID and downloadable report
- **Documentation matters as much as code** — The user guide took almost as long to write as the codebase
